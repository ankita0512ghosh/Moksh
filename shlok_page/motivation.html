Sloks have changed lives of Great People

Brihannala

In the epic Mahabharata, Arjuna, one of the Pandava warriors, spends a year in exile disguised as a eunuch named Brihannala. During this time, Brihannala teaches dance and music to the princess Uttara, showcasing artistic prowess. This narrative highlights the fluidity of gender roles and the acceptance of diverse expressions of identity within the epic.

Buchara Devi

Bahuchara Mata, also known as Bahucharaji or Bahuchari, is a Hindu goddess revered primarily in the western regions of India. She is depicted as a transgender or intersex deity. Devotees seek her blessings for fertility, protection, and liberation from gender-related struggles. The worship of Bahuchara Mata showcases a divine recognition of diverse gender identities within Hinduism.

Ardhanarishvara

Ardhanarishvara is a form of Lord Shiva that embodies the union of the divine masculine (Shiva) and feminine (Shakti) energies. This portrayal represents the inseparable nature of both aspects and the concept of non-binary existence. Ardhanarishvara exemplifies the acceptance of the fluidity and interdependence of gender identities within Hindu theology.
